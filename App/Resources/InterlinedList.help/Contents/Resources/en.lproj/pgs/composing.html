Composing and reacting

Back to Help home

Status. The composing window and the reply, dig, repost, edit, and delete actions are coming in a future update. The underlying support is in place; the visible buttons and the composer window arrive with the next milestone. This page describes how the feature will work when it ships so you can plan ahead.

Writing a new post

  1. Choose File > New Post, or press ⌘N.
  2. A separate composer window opens.
  3. Type your message. Markdown formatting is supported.
  4. Add tags by typing them with a leading #.
  5. Choose a visibility for the post.
  6. Press ⌘⏎ to publish, or close the window to discard.

Replying to a message

Open a message thread, then use the reply button at the bottom of the thread. Replies use the same composer as new posts and attach to the parent message.

"I Dig!" reactions

Click the dig button on any message to add your reaction. Click it again to remove it. The count updates immediately.

Reposting

Open the message's context menu and choose Repost to share it on your own timeline. You can add optional commentary; without commentary the repost is a plain push of the original.

Editing or deleting your own messages

From the message context menu choose Edit to change the body or tags of a post you wrote, or Delete to remove it. Deleting is permanent.

Coming in a later update

  • Image and video attachments, with automatic client-side resizing.
  • Scheduled posts.
  • Cross-posting to Mastodon, Bluesky, and LinkedIn.
